# Plan: Three lessons from my first 30 days of daily vlogging

## Structure
- **Hook (0:00–0:10):** Cold open on the most surprising or emotional lesson — no intro, no logo. State the premise ("30 days ago I started vlogging every day. Here's what actually changed.") in one breath.
- **Body (0:10–3:00):** Three lessons, each ~50–60s: setup/expectation → what actually happened → the takeaway. Order weakest-to-strongest so the video builds momentum.
- **CTA (3:00–3:20):** One clear ask (subscribe, comment your own 30-day challenge, or watch the day-1 vs day-30 comparison) tied directly to the last lesson, not bolted on.

## Shot list
1. Cold-open talking-head line delivering the hook, high energy, tight framing.
2. B-roll or clip from "day 1" footage (rough, unsure, low energy) for contrast.
3. B-roll or clip from "day 30" footage (confident, dialed-in) for contrast.
4. Talking-head for Lesson 1 — look for the take with the clearest, punchiest phrasing.
5. Talking-head for Lesson 2 — same, prioritize a clip with a natural pause before the payoff line.
6. Talking-head for Lesson 3 — the strongest emotional or funniest beat; save for last.
7. Any moment of visible failure/mistake (dropped camera, awkward silence, re-take) — great for authenticity cutaways.
8. Direct-to-camera CTA delivery, ideally with a smile or laugh for warmth.

## Talking points
1. What "daily" actually forced you to confront (consistency > perfection, or similar).
2. The gap between expected effort and actual effort (editing time, planning time, energy cost).
3. The single biggest mindset or workflow shift from day 1 to day 30.
4. Any concrete, transferable takeaway a viewer could apply themselves (not just "it was hard").
5. The CTA line, delivered with genuine tone, not a tacked-on ask.

Cut ruthlessly anything that's throat-clearing ("so um, today I wanted to talk about...") — start every point on the substance.

## Short-form angle
The **day-1 vs. day-30 contrast moment** (shots 2–3) is the best <60s vertical clip. It's visually self-explanatory even muted, doesn't require the full three-lesson setup, and has a built-in "wait, that's the same person" hook that performs well on Shorts/Reels. Pair it with a single caption card stating the single strongest lesson as text overlay, then end on the CTA. This avoids needing narrative setup — the visual contrast *is* the story.
