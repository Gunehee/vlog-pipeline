# Plan: Three lessons from my first 30 days of daily vlogging

## Structure
- **Hook (0:00–0:10)** — Cold open on the most surprising/emotional lesson, stated as a bold claim ("30 days in, and the thing that almost made me quit wasn't the camera"). No intro, no logo.
- **Body (0:10–3:00)** — Three lessons, ~40-50s each. Each beat: state the lesson → quick story/proof from footage → one-line takeaway. Order weakest-to-strongest or chronological, ending on the most quotable insight.
- **CTA (3:00–3:20)** — Direct ask (subscribe/comment which lesson resonated) tied to a specific follow-up ("day 60 update coming").
- Total runtime target: **3:00–3:30**, trimmed to 1:30–2:00 if footage is thin — cut a lesson before padding.

## Shot list
1. Talking-head cold open — energetic or raw/tired delivery, whichever is most authentic on camera
2. B-roll of the daily setup (camera, tripod, notes) establishing the "ritual" of vlogging
3. A visibly low-energy or frustrated moment (day X struggle) — proof for the honesty beat
4. A high-energy or breakthrough moment (contrast to #3)
5. Any calendar/counter/day-tracker visual (day 1 vs day 30 split-screen potential)
6. Close-up reaction shot — genuine laugh, sigh, or pause — for emotional beat and thumbnail
7. Talking-head delivering the single best one-liner/lesson, said cleanly in one take
8. Sign-off shot, direct eye contact, CTA delivery

## Talking points
1. What "30 days daily" actually required logistically (time, gear, consistency) — sets stakes
2. Lesson 1: usually about *consistency being harder than quality* — retain the specific anecdote, not just the abstract claim
3. Lesson 2: usually about *audience/self-perception shift* — what changed in how they see themselves or their content
4. Lesson 3: the most surprising/counterintuitive one — this should get the most screen time and the best line reading
5. One moment of visible doubt or near-quitting — critical for authenticity, don't cut for pacing
6. Closing takeaway that ties all three lessons into one sentence
7. Forward-looking CTA hook (what's next, day 60/90)

## Short-form angle
The **near-quitting moment paired with the counterintuitive lesson (#4/#5 above)** is the strongest <60s vertical clip. It has built-in tension (doubt → resolution) in a single self-contained arc, doesn't require the "30 days" setup to land, and the punchline lesson works as a standalone hook-payoff for scroll-stopping. Cut it as: reaction shot → 15s of the doubt story → the lesson line → quick text-on-screen recap.
