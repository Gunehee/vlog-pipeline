# Plan: Three lessons from my first 30 days of daily vlogging

## Structure
- **Hook (0:00–0:08):** Cold open on the boldest lesson or a surprising confession ("I almost quit on day 12") — no intro, no logo.
- **Body (0:08–2:45):** Three lessons, roughly 45–55s each, each with a claim → story/example → takeaway beat.
- **CTA (2:45–3:00):** One clear ask (subscribe, comment which lesson resonates, or follow the next 30-day series) delivered in the same energy as the hook, not a tonal drop-off.

## Shot list
1. Cold-open close-up with high energy / direct eye contact for the hook line.
2. B-roll of the camera setup or filming ritual (establishes "daily" premise fast).
3. Talking-head segment introducing Lesson 1.
4. Cutaway/B-roll illustrating Lesson 1's story (e.g., a missed day, a bad take).
5. Talking-head segment introducing Lesson 2, with a visible tonal or energy shift.
6. Reaction shot or pause — a moment of genuine emotion/vulnerability to use as an anchor cut.
7. Talking-head segment introducing Lesson 3.
8. Closing shot for CTA — slightly wider frame, warmer tone, direct address.

## Talking points
1. State the premise: 30 days, daily vlog, why they started.
2. Lesson 1 — likely about consistency/discipline (the hardest structural lesson).
3. Lesson 2 — likely about content/creative discovery (what actually got attention vs. what they expected).
4. Lesson 3 — likely a personal/mindset shift (how the habit changed their relationship to the camera or themselves).
5. One honest "what I'd do differently" beat — keeps it credible, not a highlight reel.
6. Clear takeaway line that ties all three lessons together in one sentence.
7. CTA tied directly to the topic (e.g., "follow along for day 60").

## Short-form angle
The vulnerability/reaction moment from shot #6 — the beat where the creator admits something real (almost quitting, a hard day, an unexpected emotional payoff) — is the strongest <60s vertical clip. It needs no setup, delivers a complete emotional arc in itself, and is the most shareable/relatable moment rather than a listicle fragment of the three lessons. Cut it as: 2-3s hook line pulled forward, the confession/story (25–35s), and the takeaway line as a punchy closer, with captions burned in for sound-off viewing.
